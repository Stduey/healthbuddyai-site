# Cross-Model Critique Prompt

Use this prompt with Claude, ChatGPT, Grok, and Reggie.

---

I am building a simple, professional public website called **HealthBuddy AI**.

The site is intended to be a plain-language education and support destination for people dealing with serious illness, palliative care needs, hard medical decisions, and common planning questions.

I want the site to feel:
- calm
- trustworthy
- medically conservative
- professionally designed
- respectful
- transparent about its limits

I do **not** want it to:
- pretend to be a hospital, government agency, or insurer
- give individualized medical advice
- use manipulative language
- overstate certainty
- center financial, estate, or billing topics
- make unsupported claims

The initial concept includes these top-level areas:
- Home
- What Palliative Care Is
- Goals of Care
- Planning Documents
- Symptom Support
- Conditions
- Videos
- Resources
- About / Transparency

Initial video topics may include:
- comfort-focused care
- ventilator / ICU
- CPR
- intubation
- defibrillation
- full code cascade

Initial condition pages may include:
- cancer
- heart failure
- COPD
- dementia
- kidney disease

Planning document pages may include:
- POLST
- advance directives
- DNR / DNI basics
- healthcare proxy / medical decision maker

The site may also include conservative, widely known supportive measures for symptoms, framed carefully and not as substitutes for medical care. Examples might include hydration support for constipation when appropriate, leg elevation for swelling when appropriate, ginger tea for nausea when appropriate, repositioning and skin protection reminders, and cool airflow / upright positioning for shortness of breath when appropriate.

I want your help stress-testing this concept before building the site.

## Your task
Please give me a serious critique of this plan.

Specifically answer these questions:

1. What are the **biggest strategic risks** in this website concept?
2. What parts of the concept are **strongest** and most likely to build trust?
3. What pages or sections are **missing**?
4. What parts should be **removed, narrowed, or delayed** for the initial launch?
5. How can the site look **professional and highly credible** without falsely appearing “official”?
6. What would make clinicians more comfortable sharing this site with patients and families?
7. What homepage framing would best communicate the site’s purpose in a way that feels respectful and trustworthy?
8. What legal/clinical trust issues should be handled carefully in wording, structure, or disclaimers?
9. Which elements are most likely to create **maintenance burden** or **accuracy drift** over time?
10. If you had to define the **best possible version** of this site in one paragraph, how would you describe it?

## Important constraints
- Keep the recommendations practical.
- Prefer simple over complex.
- Prefer trustworthiness over comprehensiveness.
- Prefer clarity over ambition.
- Assume this is the first version of a larger long-term platform.
- Do not suggest flashy growth tactics or manipulative copy.
- Keep the voice aligned with serious illness, palliative care, and patient/family education.

Please be direct. I want useful criticism, not praise.
