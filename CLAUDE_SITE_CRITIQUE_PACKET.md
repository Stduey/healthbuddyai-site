# Site Brief

## Purpose
Create a simple, professional, plain-language public website for **HealthBuddy AI**.

This website should function as the public-facing educational and credibility layer for the HealthBuddy brand. It should introduce HealthBuddy clearly, explain what the project is for, and offer genuinely useful plain-language educational content for patients, families, and caregivers dealing with serious illness, chronic illness burden, palliative care questions, and hard medical decisions.

This is not a replacement for a doctor, nurse, hospice team, or emergency care. It is a general education destination that helps patients and families better understand serious illness, common symptoms, goals-of-care decisions, planning documents, supportive care concepts, and future video-based education.

## Core Positioning
HealthBuddy AI is a plain-language education brand for serious illness, complex chronic illness, palliative care support, and hard medical decisions.

The website should present as:
- the public-facing home of the HealthBuddy brand
- professional
- transparent
- medically conservative
- emotionally respectful
- useful to patients and families
- appropriate for clinicians to share

The site should NOT present as:
- a hospital
- a government agency
- a law firm
- an insurer
- a substitute for medical care
- a source of individualized medical advice

## Strategic Framing Question
One of the main things I need help deciding is how the website should frame HealthBuddy at launch.

The broader HealthBuddy vision includes:
- plain-language patient education
- serious illness and chronic illness support
- symptom and supportive care education
- goals-of-care and informed-decision education
- future video-based education as a major pillar

I need your critique on whether the website should launch primarily as:
- a **HealthBuddy brand / credibility site** with selected educational content
- a **public education library** under the HealthBuddy brand
- a **video-first goals-of-care education site** under the HealthBuddy brand
- or a carefully layered combination of those approaches

## Audience
Primary audience:
- patients with serious illness
- family members and caregivers
- people navigating palliative care, hospice, code status, or advance care planning questions

Secondary audience:
- clinicians looking for simple educational material to share

## Trust Rules
These rules are central to the brand.

1. Use plain language.
2. Stick to generally accepted, commonly known medical facts.
3. Do not overstate certainty.
4. Do not use manipulative copy.
5. Do not present financial, estate, or billing guidance as a site focus.
6. Do not imply institutional affiliation that does not exist.
7. Be explicit that the site is educational, not personal medical advice.
8. Encourage users to discuss important decisions and worsening symptoms with their own clinicians.
9. Do not include protected health information or require it for basic site use.
10. Use transparent, humble language about limits.

## Tone
- calm
- compassionate
- clear
- restrained
- professional
- dignified
- non-sensational
- honest

Avoid:
- hype
- startup jargon
- aggressive marketing language
- fear-based persuasion
- false urgency
- anything that feels exploitative

## Site Goals
1. Establish HealthBuddy AI as a credible, trustworthy public education brand.
2. Help people understand palliative care, serious illness support, and chronic illness burden in plain language.
3. Help people understand code status and goals-of-care decisions.
4. Explain common planning documents like POLST and advance directives.
5. Offer basic symptom education and supportive measures.
6. Offer plain-language condition overviews for major serious illnesses and common chronic disease burdens.
7. Provide carefully chosen practical support links and contact information for public programs and major insurers where useful.
8. Create a strong foundation for future personalized, de-identified education.
9. Make video education a central feature of the experience.

## Launch Scope
Initial launch should stay intentionally simple and polished.

- Home
- What Palliative Care Is
- Goals of Care
- Planning Documents
- Symptom Support
- Conditions
- Videos
- Resources
- About / Transparency

## Suggested Information Architecture

### Home
Purpose:
- explain what the site is
- explain who it is for
- highlight video education
- point users to the main sections quickly

Suggested homepage sections:
- Hero
- What this site helps with
- Featured videos
- Browse by topic
- Browse by condition
- Planning documents
- Support resources
- Transparency statement

### What Palliative Care Is
Explain:
- what palliative care means
- symptom relief and quality of life
- support during serious illness
- that palliative care can be provided alongside treatment
- that it is not the same thing as “giving up”

### Goals of Care
Create plain-language pages for:
- What goals of care means
- What full code means
- CPR basics
- Intubation and ventilator basics
- Comfort-focused care
- Questions families can ask the medical team

### Planning Documents
Create plain-language pages for:
- POLST
- Advance directives
- Healthcare proxy / medical decision maker
- DNR / DNI basics
- How these documents differ

### Symptom Support
Create general information pages for:
- pain
- shortness of breath
- nausea
- constipation
- diarrhea
- poor appetite
- swelling / edema
- skin protection / pressure injury prevention
- fatigue / weakness
- confusion / delirium basics

Each symptom page should use a simple structure:
- what it is
- common causes
- what people may notice
- basic supportive measures
- when to contact the care team
- urgent warning signs

### Conditions
Start with a focused set:
- cancer
- heart failure
- COPD
- dementia
- kidney disease

Each condition page should include:
- what it is
- common symptoms
- common treatment burdens
- common management challenges
- what families often struggle with
- how palliative care may help

### Videos
Videos should be a major part of the site.

Initial priority videos:
- comfort-focused care
- ventilator / ICU
- CPR
- intubation
- defibrillation
- full code cascade

Video presentation should feel educational, calm, and respectful.

### Resources
Separate this from clinical education.

Possible resources:
- Medicare
- Medicaid
- major insurance contact pages or member support pages
- hospice and palliative care organizations
- planning document resources

Note: external contact data changes frequently. The design should make these easy to update.

### About / Transparency
This page should make the site more credible by being direct about what it is and what it is not.

Suggested content:
- mission
- who the site is for
- educational purpose
- limits of content
- not emergency care
- not personal medical advice
- encourage discussion with licensed clinicians

## Supportive Measures Policy
The site may include conservative, widely known supportive measures when framed carefully.

Examples:
- ice or heat for pain when appropriate
- leg elevation for swelling when appropriate
- hydration support for constipation when appropriate
- ginger tea for nausea when appropriate
- repositioning and skin protection reminders
- cool airflow and upright positioning for shortness of breath when appropriate

Rules:
- keep them conservative
- do not position them as treatment substitutes
- avoid dosing or medication advice
- include a brief reminder to check with a clinician, especially if symptoms worsen or if restrictions apply

## Design Direction
The site should look:
- modern
- clean
- calm
- accessible
- medically respectful
- high trust

Visual direction:
- soft neutrals
- restrained blue/green accents
- generous whitespace
- clear headings
- easy reading
- subtle icons if needed
- avoid flashy gradients, gimmicks, and startup aesthetics

The site should feel “professionally designed,” but not mimic hospital systems or government sites.

## Content Guardrails
Do not include:
- individualized medical advice
- specific medication dosing guidance
- unsupported claims
- fake citations
- legal or estate planning guidance as a core content area
- pressure tactics
- miracle language
- exaggerated claims of authority

## Technical Build Preferences
- static HTML/CSS site or equally lightweight approach
- no backend for the first version
- no framework requirement for the first version if simple HTML/CSS is enough
- fast load times
- mobile-friendly
- accessible typography and spacing
- easy to expand later
- easy to connect future videos and deeper condition-specific education

## Success Criteria
The site succeeds if it:
1. immediately feels trustworthy and respectful
2. is understandable to patients and families
3. is simple enough for clinicians to share without embarrassment
4. creates a strong foundation for future personalized education
5. looks professional without pretending to be an institution
6. organizes hard topics in a way that reduces confusion

## North Star
Build a site that helps ordinary people understand serious illness with more clarity, dignity, and honesty than they usually get online.
